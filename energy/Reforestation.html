Reforestation Areas Project

Environmental Impact Assessment and Monitoring

Project Overview

This project was carried out in the Vichada department, Colombia, with the aim of revalidating the ecological potential of the region and monitoring its progress. To achieve this, the Verra methodology 0047 was applied, providing a standardized framework for reforestation. Landsat satellite data and the Normalized Difference Forest Index (NDFI) were used to assess and track the progress of the selected restoration areas.

Methodology

We employed satellite imagery and geospatial data analysis techniques to identify areas with significant Potential. The key steps in the project include:

  • Data Collection: The most optimal Landsat images were selected, ensuring minimal cloud coverage and consistency in timing (same months, etc.).
  • Stoking Index Analysis: The NDFI was generated for each year following the methodology proposed by Carlos M. Souza Jr.
  • Monitoring and Feedback: Ongoing monitoring of reforestation sites to evaluate the success of planting efforts and their environmental impact.

Results & Impact

As a result of this project, several deforested regions have been identified and mapped for reforestation. The key outcomes include:

  • Identified 500+ hectares of land suitable for reforestation in the target region.
  • Collaboration with local governments to implement planting strategies and track reforestation progress.
  • Developed predictive models to assess the potential success of reforestation efforts based on environmental data.
  • Raised awareness about the importance of reforestation in mitigating climate change and protecting biodiversity.
